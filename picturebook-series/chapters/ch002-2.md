# 第002-2章：西施的眉头和山谷的回声

## 典故引入

### 第1页

庄子讲过一个故事：春秋时有位女子叫西施，大家都说她很美。有一天她心口疼，轻轻按着胸口，眉头也皱了起来。

路人看见，说她这样也动人。那不是摆出来的美，而是从她真实的身体里流出来的样子。

### 第2页

同村的东施看见了，以为“按胸口、皱眉头”就是美。她也照着做，还故意走给大家看。

人们没有觉得自然，反而吓得躲开。这个故事后来叫“东施效颦”。

### 第3页

这个故事不是为了嘲笑谁长得不好。它提醒我们：只学别人的外样，却不明白它为什么自然，就会越用力越不像自己。

这正好能照见第二章开头：一旦大家死守一种“美”，许多“不美”就被挤出来了。

## 正文

### 第4页

阿禾听完故事，办了一场“最美皱眉大会”。小兔皱眉，小鸟皱眉，小石头也努力皱眉。

没多久，大家的脸都酸了。整个院子像下了一场不开心的雨。

### 第5页

忽然，小豆苗从泥里探出新叶。小兔忘了皱眉，咧嘴笑了起来。

阿禾看着他的笑，心里一亮。原来最动人的样子，常常不是装出来的。

### 第6页

老人说：“西施的眉头动人，是因为那一刻是真的。东施错在把别人的自然，硬贴到自己脸上。”

阿禾点点头，把“最美皱眉大会”的牌子收了起来。

### 第7页

下午，他们走到山谷口。小石头拍手，啪，一声飞出去。

山谷慢慢还回来：啪。孩子们都笑了，原来声音也有朋友。

### 第8页

小鸟吹一声高音，小兔敲一声低鼓。高的声音没有压住低的，低的声音也没有抢走高的。

它们一来一往，竟然像搭起一座小桥。

### 第9页

古书《礼记·乐记》说，心被外物触动，就发出声。声和声相应，变化成有节奏的样子，就成了音。

阿禾听懂了一点：一个声音独自响着，还不是音乐。

### 第10页

他们试着只敲一只鼓，咚，咚，咚。声音很响，却有点孤单。

再加上竹笛、石片和拍手，声音开始互相让路。小曲子从中间长了出来。

### 第11页

阿禾想抢着当领头的。老人只把手放在耳边，做了一个“听”的动作。

阿禾停下来，先听大家。等到空处出现，他才轻轻敲了一下。

### 第12页

那一下不大，却刚好接住前一声。大家没有争谁最响，曲子反而更好听。

风从山谷里吹出来，把最后一点回声送回孩子们身边。

## 回扣

### 第13页

第二章说“美”和“不美”相对而生，也说“音声相和”。西施和东施的故事，让阿禾看见：固定一种美，容易逼人模仿。

山谷里的声音又告诉他：不同的声相互应和，才会成乐。

### 第14页

所以“无为”不是不管，而是不把自己的标准硬压上去。让花像花，让笑像笑，让每个声音找到自己的位置。

事情成了，也不急着说“都是我的功劳”。音乐留下来，比名字更重要。

### 第15页

阿禾在本子上写：不要把一种样子叫作全部的美。不要让一个声音盖住所有声音。

我愿意先听一听，再轻轻加入。这样，世界也许会自己唱起来。

# Chapter 002-2: Xi Shi's Brow and the Echo in the Valley

## Allusion

### Page 1

Zhuangzi tells a story about Xi Shi, a woman people in ancient China called beautiful. One day her chest hurt, so she pressed her hand to her heart and gently frowned.

People passing by still found her moving. It was not a pose; it grew from what was truly happening in her body.

### Page 2

Dong Shi, who lived in the same village, saw this and thought, "So pressing the chest and frowning is beauty." She copied the gesture and walked out to be seen.

People did not feel natural beauty; they hid away in alarm. The story became known as "Dong Shi imitates the frown."

### Page 3

This story is not for mocking someone's looks. It reminds us that copying another person's outside shape, without the living truth inside it, makes us less like ourselves.

That fits the opening of Chapter Two: once everyone clings to one form of "beauty," many things get pushed into "not beautiful."

## Main Text

### Page 4

After hearing the story, Ahe held a "Most Beautiful Frown Contest." Little Rabbit frowned, Little Bird frowned, and Little Stone tried very hard to frown.

Soon everyone's face ached. The yard felt as if an unhappy rain had fallen.

### Page 5

Then a bean sprout pushed a new leaf out of the soil. Little Rabbit forgot to frown and broke into a grin.

Ahe saw the smile and felt something brighten. The most moving face is often not the one we perform.

### Page 6

The old man said, "Xi Shi's frown moved people because it was true in that moment. Dong Shi's mistake was pasting someone else's natural gesture onto her own face."

Ahe nodded and quietly took down the contest sign.

### Page 7

That afternoon, they walked to the valley. Little Stone clapped once: clap. The sound flew out.

The valley slowly returned it: clap. The children laughed; even a sound could have a friend.

### Page 8

Little Bird blew a high note, and Little Rabbit tapped a low drum. The high note did not crush the low one, and the low one did not steal the high.

Back and forth they went, like a small bridge made of sound.

### Page 9

The ancient *Record of Music* says: when the heart is stirred by things outside, it sends out sound. When sounds answer one another and form a pattern, they become music.

Ahe understood a little: one sound ringing alone is not yet music.

### Page 10

They tried only one drum: dong, dong, dong. It was loud, but lonely.

Then they added bamboo pipe, stone chips, and clapping hands. The sounds made room for one another, and a small tune grew between them.

### Page 11

Ahe wanted to rush in as leader. The old man only cupped one hand to his ear, making the sign for "listen."

Ahe stopped and listened first. When a little space opened, he tapped once, softly.

### Page 12

The tap was not big, but it caught the sound before it. No one fought to be loudest, and the tune became lovelier.

Wind came out of the valley and carried the last echo back to the children.

## Return to the Original

### Page 13

Chapter Two says beauty and not-beauty arise in relation, and that notes and tones become harmonious together. Xi Shi and Dong Shi show Ahe how one fixed beauty can force people into copying.

The valley sounds show him another thing: different voices answering one another can become music.

### Page 14

So "without doing anything" does not mean neglect. It means not pressing your own standard onto everything: let a flower be a flower, a smile be a smile, and each sound find its place.

When the work is done, do not hurry to say, "That was all mine." The music matters more than the name.

### Page 15

Ahe wrote in his notebook: Do not call one shape the whole of beauty. Do not let one sound cover every other sound.

I will listen first, then join softly. Perhaps the world will begin to sing by itself.
