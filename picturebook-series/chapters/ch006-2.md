# 第006-2章：沉默里的声音

## 典故引入

### 第1页

1952年，作曲家约翰·凯奇让钢琴家走上舞台，坐在琴前，却没有弹下一个音。

观众等了很久，先是不安，后来慢慢听见了别的声音。

### 第2页

有人听见椅子轻响，有人听见风从厅外经过，也有人听见自己的呼吸。

原来沉默不是空白。沉默像山谷，让声音自己出现。

### 第3页

第六章说“谷神不死”，正可以这样理解。空处不是死处，而是能让万物显现的地方。

琴键没有被敲响，音乐却从安静里露出根来。

### 第4页

古希腊的阿基米德，曾为一顶王冠想破了头。国王要他判断王冠是否掺了别的金属，又不能把王冠弄坏。

他画图、计算、苦想，越急越卡住。

### 第5页

一天，他去洗澡，身体进水，水从盆边漫出来。他忽然明白：溢出的水，可以告诉他王冠占了多少地方。

答案不是在更用力的时候来，而是在心松开的一刻冒出来。

### 第6页

这不是说不用学习。阿基米德早已想了很多，心里才有能接住答案的山谷。

“用之不勤”是别把弦拉到断。用过之后，给心一点空。

### 第7页

澳大利亚许多原住民传统里，有“歌之径”。长路不只画在纸上，也藏在一首首歌里。

歌里记着山岗、树、沙地和水洼。

### 第8页

旅人唱到哪里，脚步就知道往哪里走。歌声细细延续，像一条看不见的路。

它不把土地踩成伤口，却让人知道泉水在哪里、方向在哪里。

### 第9页

“绵绵若存”就像这样的歌。你看不见路牌，却能跟着旋律走很远。

真正的根，不一定插在眼前；有时它藏在记忆、呼吸和声音之间。

### 第10页

美国西南部有科罗拉多大峡谷。它不是被一把巨斧劈开的。

科罗拉多河在漫长岁月里流过，日复一日，年复一年，慢慢把岩石磨出深谷。

### 第11页

水看起来柔，时间看起来慢。可柔水不停，慢也会有大力量。

这也是“绵绵”：不急着证明，却从不真正断掉。

### 第12页

中国《易经》说，安静到极处，遇到事物便能通达。像平静的池面，叶子落下，涟漪立刻知道。

安静不是迟钝。安静是没有乱波，所以能回应。

### 第13页

临济禅师也借过一个比喻：锋利的刀，用过之后也要磨养。

再好用的心，若一直催它、耗它，也会钝。会休息，才会长久清明。

## 回扣

### 第14页

凯奇的沉默，阿基米德的放松，歌之径的绵延，大峡谷的柔水，都在讲第六章。

空不是没有，静不是死，慢不是无力。

### 第15页

谷神像安静的山谷，什么也不抓，却能让声音、水和路出现。

玄牝像深处的母源，什么也不炫，却让生命有门、有根。

### 第16页

所以，别把心用得太勤。读书要认真，也要休息；帮助人要真诚，也要宽舒。

让心有一点山谷的空，生命的泉水才会绵绵地流。

# Chapter 006-2: Sounds Inside Silence

## Allusion

### Page 1

In 1952, the composer John Cage had a pianist walk onto a stage and sit at the piano without playing a single note.

The audience waited for a long time. First they grew uneasy; then they began to hear other sounds.

### Page 2

Some heard chairs creak. Some heard wind outside the hall. Some heard their own breathing.

Silence was not blank. It was like a valley, letting sounds appear by themselves.

### Page 3

This helps us understand "the valley spirit dies not." Empty space is not dead space; it lets the ten thousand things show themselves.

No piano key was struck, yet music showed a root inside stillness.

### Page 4

In ancient Greece, Archimedes struggled over a king's crown. The king wanted to know whether cheaper metal had been mixed in, but the crown could not be damaged.

Archimedes drew, calculated, and worried. The harder he pushed, the more stuck he felt.

### Page 5

One day, he stepped into a bath and water spilled over the edge. Suddenly he saw that displaced water could show how much space the crown took up.

The answer did not arrive when he pushed harder. It surfaced when the mind loosened.

### Page 6

This does not mean we should avoid study. Archimedes had already thought deeply, so his mind had a valley ready to receive the answer.

"Used gently" means: do not pull the string until it breaks. After using the mind, give it space.

### Page 7

In many Australian Aboriginal traditions, there are Songlines. Long paths are not only drawn on paper; they are carried in songs.

The songs remember hills, trees, sand, and waterholes.

### Page 8

As travelers sing, their feet know where to go. The song continues softly, like an invisible road.

It does not wound the land, yet it tells people where water is and where direction lies.

### Page 9

"Long and unbroken" is like such a song. You may not see a signpost, yet a melody can guide you far.

A true root is not always before the eyes. Sometimes it lives among memory, breath, and sound.

### Page 10

In the American Southwest lies the Grand Canyon. It was not opened by a giant axe.

The Colorado River flowed through it over vast time, day after day and year after year, slowly shaping rock into a deep valley.

### Page 11

Water looks soft, and time looks slow. But soft water that does not stop can have great power.

This too is "unbroken": not rushing to prove itself, yet never truly cut off.

### Page 12

The Chinese *Yijing* says that when stillness is deep, it can respond when touched. Like a quiet pond, it knows the falling leaf at once.

Stillness is not dullness. Stillness has no confused waves, so it can respond.

### Page 13

The Chan master Linji also used a sharp image: even a fine blade must be cared for after it is used.

Even a clear mind grows dull if we keep driving and draining it. Rest helps clarity last.

## Return to the Original

### Page 14

Cage's silence, Archimedes' loosened mind, the Songlines' continuity, and the river in the canyon all speak to Chapter Six.

Emptiness is not nothing. Stillness is not death. Slowness is not weakness.

### Page 15

The valley spirit is like a quiet valley: it grasps nothing, yet sound, water, and paths appear through it.

The female mystery is like a deep mother-source: it displays nothing, yet life has a gate and a root there.

### Page 16

So do not overuse the heart. Study seriously, and also rest. Help sincerely, and also stay spacious.

Give the heart a little valley-like emptiness. Then the spring of life can keep flowing softly.
