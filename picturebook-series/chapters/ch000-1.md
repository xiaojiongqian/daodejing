# 第000-1章：一个爱看水的老人

## 第1页

这一天，阿禾坐在村口大树下，没有急着翻开"名字本"。

他问老人："道可道，非常道，是谁先说出来的？"

老人望着远山说："一个爱看水的老人。他叫老子。"

## 第2页

"老子是名字吗？"小兔问。

老人说："有人说他姓李，名耳，也叫老聃。'老子'是尊称，意思是有智慧的老先生。"

史书记得很少，传说却很多。我们要把两件事分清。

## 第3页

老子大约活在两千五百年前，比秦始皇修长城还早。

那是东周末年的乱世。路上有兵车，田边有兵器。

越吵的年代，越有人坐下来，想一想世界到底该怎样安定。

## 第4页

老人在线上画了许多小圈。

"这些是东周的诸侯国。齐、晋、楚、秦，还有后来的韩、赵、魏、燕，都想让别人听自己的。"

小鸟说："像我们争大象叫什么？"

老人说："更严重。他们争天下。"

## 第5页

"老子做什么工作？"阿禾问。

"他在周朝守国家藏书。"老人说，"那时的书写在竹简上，很重，也很少。"

有人来查书，他帮忙找。没有人来时，他就安静地读。

## 第6页

老子读历史、诗歌、礼法，也读战争的记录。

可是他不只看书。窗外的树、河水、路人和马蹄声，他也看。

老人说："懂道的人，不能只看字，还要看世界。"

## 第7页

老人带孩子们走到小溪边。

"你们看水。石头挡住它，它就绕过去；悬崖挡住它，它就落下去。"

"它不吵，也不争，可是谁也不能让它永远停住。"

## 第8页

"老子也看树。"老人说。

"大树一开始只是软软的小芽。它不喊自己强大，只是一年一年往上长。"

阿禾写下两个字：水，树。他觉得这两个字好像会呼吸。

## 第9页

老人放低声音："关于老子的出生，有一个神奇传说。"

"传说不是历史事实。它像一件发光的衣服，披在真实的人身上。"

小鸟立刻说："那也想听。"

## 第10页

传说里，老子的母亲怀他八十一年。

他出生时，头发胡子都白了，还生在李树下，所以姓李，叫老子。

小兔笑了。老人也笑："这大概不是历史，却说明后人觉得他智慧很老。"

## 第11页

"他见过孔子吗？"小石头问。

老人说："《史记》里记着，孔子曾去问老子关于礼的事。"

老子提醒他：旧规矩留下了字，可制定规矩的人早已远去。

## 第12页

孔子回来后，说自己见到了像龙一样的人。

"鸟会飞，鱼会游，兽会跑，我都知道。可是龙乘风上天，我不知道它怎样做到。"

阿禾想：原来真正深的东西，看得见一点，却装不进一个名字。

## 第13页

后来，老子看见周朝越来越乱。

书看够了，水也看够了，人世的争夺也看够了。

传说他骑上一头青牛，慢慢往西边走去。

## 第14页

"为什么骑牛，不骑马？"小兔问。

老人笑了："马太快。急着逃的人骑马，不急着争的人骑牛。"

阿禾点点头："所以老子不是逃。他只是走。"

## 第15页

傍晚，阿禾在本子上写：

*老子，一个爱看水的老人。史书很少，传说很多。他像龙，看得见鳞片，看不见全身。*

老人说："明天，我们讲那些字怎样走到今天。"

# Chapter 000-1: An Old Man Who Loved to Watch Water

## Page 1

That day, Ahe sat beneath the big tree by the village gate. He did not hurry to open his "book of names."

He asked the old man, "Who first said, 'The Dao that can be trodden is not the enduring Dao'?"

The old man looked toward the mountains. "An old man who loved to watch water. His name was Laozi."

## Page 2

"Is Laozi a name?" Little Rabbit asked.

"Some say his family name was Li, and his given name was Er. Some call him Lao Dan," the old man said. "'Laozi' is a title of respect. It means a wise old master."

History records little. Legend tells much. We must keep the two apart.

## Page 3

Laozi lived about two thousand five hundred years ago, long before the First Emperor built the Great Wall.

It was a noisy age near the end of the Eastern Zhou. War carts rolled on roads; weapons stood near fields.

In the loudest times, some people sit down and ask how the world might become still.

## Page 4

The old man drew many circles along a line.

"These are the states of the Eastern Zhou. Qi, Jin, Chu, Qin, and later Han, Zhao, Wei, and Yan all wanted others to obey."

"Like us arguing over the elephant's name?" Little Bird asked.

"Worse," said the old man. "They argued over all under heaven."

## Page 5

"What work did Laozi do?" Ahe asked.

"He kept the royal books of Zhou," said the old man. "Books were written on bamboo slips then. They were heavy, and each one held little."

When people came, he helped them find records. When no one came, he read quietly.

## Page 6

Laozi read histories, poems, rites, and records of war.

But he did not only look at books. He watched trees, water, travelers, and the sound of hooves outside the window.

"A person who seeks Dao cannot look only at words," said the old man. "He must also look at the world."

## Page 7

The old man led the children to the stream.

"Look at water. When a stone blocks it, it flows around. When a cliff stops it, it falls."

"It does not shout or fight. Yet no one can make it stop forever."

## Page 8

"Laozi watched trees too," the old man said.

"A great tree begins as a soft sprout. It does not announce its strength. It simply grows, year after year."

Ahe wrote two words: water, tree. The words seemed almost to breathe.

## Page 9

The old man lowered his voice. "There is a strange legend about Laozi's birth."

"A legend is not historical fact. It is like a shining robe placed over a real person."

Little Bird said at once, "I still want to hear it."

## Page 10

In the legend, Laozi's mother carried him for eighty-one years.

When he was born, his hair and beard were already white. He was born under a plum tree, so his family name was Li.

Little Rabbit laughed. The old man laughed too. "It is probably not history. It tells us people felt his wisdom was very old."

## Page 11

"Did he meet Confucius?" Little Stone asked.

"The *Records of the Grand Historian* says Confucius once asked Laozi about rites," said the old man.

Laozi reminded him: old rules leave words behind, but the people who made them have long gone.

## Page 12

Afterward, Confucius said he had met someone like a dragon.

"Birds fly, fish swim, beasts run. I know these. But a dragon rides wind and clouds into the sky. I do not know how."

Ahe thought: the deepest things can be glimpsed, but not held by one name.

## Page 13

Later, Laozi saw the Zhou world grow more chaotic.

He had read enough books, watched enough water, and seen enough human struggle.

Legend says he mounted a blue-green ox and slowly went west.

## Page 14

"Why ride an ox, not a horse?" Little Rabbit asked.

The old man smiled. "A horse is too fast. One who flees rides a horse. One who no longer competes may ride an ox."

Ahe nodded. "So Laozi was not fleeing. He was simply leaving."

## Page 15

At dusk, Ahe wrote in his notebook:

*Laozi, an old man who loved water. History says little; legends say much. He was like a dragon. You can glimpse the scales, but not the whole body.*

"Tomorrow," said the old man, "we will hear how those words reached us."
