# 第012-3章：塞壬的歌声

## 典故引入

### 第1页

荷马史诗《奥德赛》里，有一段很有名的海上故事。

特洛伊战争结束后，奥德修斯带着水手回家。他们已经离家很久，最想看见的是故乡的屋顶、港口和亲人的脸。

### 第2页

可是归家的海路上，有一座危险的岛。

岛上有塞壬。传说她们的歌声美得让人忘记一切。船只一靠近，水手就会被歌声牵着走，忘了礁石，忘了船，也忘了自己本来要回家。

### 第3页

奥德修斯想听那歌声。

他不是不知道危险。正因为知道，他没有对自己说：“我意志很强，听一听也没事。”

### 第4页

他做了一件很奇怪、也很聪明的事。

他让水手们用蜡封住耳朵，这样他们听不见歌声。又让他们把自己绑在桅杆上，绑得结结实实。

### 第5页

他还提前吩咐：“等会儿不管我怎么喊、怎么求，你们都不要给我松绑。”

水手们点头。那时海面很平，风也正好。危险还没来，人的心比较清楚。

### 第6页

船慢慢靠近塞壬岛。

远处先是一点声音，像银线落到水面。接着歌声越来越近，甜得像蜜，又亮得像刀背上的光。

### 第7页

奥德修斯听见了。

他的眼神变了。他忘了自己刚才怎样安排，忘了船旁边可能有礁石，也忘了故乡还在前面。

### 第8页

他拼命挣扎，喊水手给他解开。

水手们听不见歌声，只看见船长在桅杆上发急。他们记得先前的约定，没有松手，反而把绳子绑得更紧。

### 第9页

歌声还在追。

奥德修斯的身体向海那边扑，绳子勒住他的胸口。那一刻，他不是没有耳朵；他是耳朵被歌声占满，心听不见回家的路了。

### 第10页

船继续往前。

礁石从船边退到后面，歌声一点一点远去。等水手们取下耳中的蜡，海面又只剩桨声和风声。

### 第11页

他们这才解开奥德修斯。

他喘着气，低头看身上的绳痕。那些绳子刚才让他难受，却也把他留在了活着的船上。

### 第12页

小石头听到这里，皱起眉：“他不是英雄吗？英雄也会被歌声骗走？”

老人说：“所以他才聪明。他知道自己也会糊涂，就趁清醒的时候，先给糊涂的自己留下一条绳子。”

### 第13页

阿禾想起集市上的鼓声和蓝珠子。

那天他也不是没有耳朵。小石头明明叫过他。只是叫卖声太近、太亮，把朋友的声音挤到很远。

### 第14页

老人说：“有些声音，会把人从自己的路上叫走。”

“真正会听的人，不是什么都听进去。他知道哪些声音可以听，哪些声音要隔远一点。”

## 回扣

### 第15页

第十二章说“五音令人耳聋”。

塞壬的歌声不是让耳朵坏掉，而是让耳朵太满，满到听不见礁石、同伴和回家的方向。

### 第16页

“驰骋畋猎令人心发狂”，也可以这样看。

人被刺激追着跑时，会把眼前的甜声当成全部。心一发狂，就忘了自己原本要去哪里。

### 第17页

“圣人为腹不为目”，在海上就是：先保住船，保住身体，保住回家的路。

歌声再美，也不能拿全船人的性命去换。

### 第18页

阿禾写下：我不能只相信当时的自己。

清醒的时候，要给容易被带走的自己留一条绳子。有些歌很好听，可我的路，比那一刻的好听更重要。

# Chapter 012-3: The Sirens' Song

## Allusion

### Page 1

In Homer's Odyssey, there is a famous story at sea.

After the Trojan War, Odysseus and his sailors were sailing home. They had been away for a long time. What they most wanted to see was the roof of home, the harbor, and the faces of those waiting for them.

### Page 2

But on the way home, there was a dangerous island.

There lived the Sirens. Their song was said to be so beautiful that people forgot everything. When ships came near, sailors followed the sound and forgot rocks, ship, and the home they meant to reach.

### Page 3

Odysseus wanted to hear the song.

He did know the danger. Because he knew it, he did not say to himself, "My will is strong. I can listen and be fine."

### Page 4

He did something strange and wise.

He had the sailors seal their ears with wax so they could not hear the song. Then he ordered them to bind him firmly to the mast.

### Page 5

He told them beforehand, "No matter how I shout or beg later, do not untie me."

The sailors nodded. At that time, the sea was calm and the wind was good. Danger had not arrived yet, so the heart could still think clearly.

### Page 6

The ship slowly came near the Sirens' island.

At first, the sound was far away, like a silver thread touching the water. Then the song came closer, sweet as honey and bright as light on a blade.

### Page 7

Odysseus heard it.

His eyes changed. He forgot his own plan, forgot the rocks beside the ship, and forgot that home still lay ahead.

### Page 8

He struggled hard and shouted for the sailors to untie him.

The sailors could not hear the song. They only saw their captain straining against the mast. Remembering his earlier command, they did not loosen the ropes. They tied them tighter.

### Page 9

The song kept following.

Odysseus threw his body toward the sea, and the ropes held his chest. In that moment, he did not lack ears. His ears were so full of song that the heart could no longer hear the road home.

### Page 10

The ship kept moving.

The rocks slipped behind them, and the song slowly faded. When the sailors took the wax from their ears, the sea held only oars and wind.

### Page 11

Only then did they untie Odysseus.

He breathed hard and looked down at the marks of the rope. The ropes had hurt him, but they had kept him alive on the ship.

### Page 12

Little Stone frowned. "Wasn't he a hero? Can a hero be fooled by a song too?"

The old man said, "That is why he was wise. He knew he could become confused, so while he was clear, he left a rope for the confused self."

### Page 13

Ahe remembered the market drums and the blue bead.

That day, he had not lacked ears. Little Stone had called him. But the seller's voice was too near and too bright, pushing his friend's voice far away.

### Page 14

The old man said, "Some sounds call people away from their own road."

"A person who truly knows how to listen does not let every sound enter. He knows which sounds to hear, and which sounds to keep at a distance."

## Return

### Page 15

Chapter Twelve says that many notes can make the ears deaf.

The Sirens' song did not break the ears. It filled them so completely that they could not hear rocks, companions, or the direction home.

### Page 16

The line about chasing excitement until the heart grows wild can also be read this way.

When people are pulled by thrill, the sweet sound in front becomes everything. Once the heart goes wild, it forgets where it meant to go.

### Page 17

"For the belly, not for the eyes" at sea means: first protect the ship, the body, and the road home.

No song, however beautiful, is worth the lives of the whole crew.

### Page 18

Ahe wrote: I cannot trust only the self of the moment.

When clear, I must leave a rope for the self that may be carried away. Some songs are beautiful, but my road is more important than one moment of beauty.
