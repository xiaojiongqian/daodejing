# 第015-3章：浑水慢慢清

## 典故引入

### 第1页

第十五章有一句很适合放在杯子边看：

“孰能浊以静之徐清？”谁能让浑水安静下来，慢慢变清？

### 第2页

暴雨后，空空村的小池塘变成了黄泥色。

水面漂着断草，泥沙在底下翻，连平时爱照影子的白鹅，也站在岸边不肯下去。

### 第3页

阿禾想帮池水快点清。

他拿来一根长竿，伸进水里搅，想把草叶挑出来。竿子一动，池底的泥又翻上来，水更浑了。

### 第4页

小石头拿来布袋，说：“我们把水滤一遍。”

他们舀起一桶水倒进布袋。细泥从布缝里慢慢渗出来，手上、鞋上、地上，全成了黄的。

### 第5页

忙了半天，池塘没有清，孩子们倒像从泥里捞出来的。

阿禾有点恼：“为什么越帮越乱？”

### 第6页

老人没有笑他们。

他只舀了一碗浑水，放在石阶上：“先看这一碗。”

### 第7页

一开始，水里什么都在动。

泥点像小小的云，草屑上下浮。阿禾忍不住想碰，老人摇头：“别急。”

### 第8页

过了一会儿，重的泥慢慢沉下去。

水上面先清了一点，接着中间也亮了一点。碗底聚起一层细细的黄泥。

### 第9页

阿禾小声说：“它自己会清。”

老人说：“不是不用管。是先别乱管。浑的时候，最怕急手。”

### 第10页

他们把池塘边的进水口清开，让新的雨水能慢慢流出去。

又把被风吹来的断枝捞走，只做必要的事，不再把长竿插到池底乱搅。

### 第11页

第二天，池水还是黄的，但浅处已经能看见石头。

第三天，白鹅试着下水，脚掌一划，水纹散开，没再翻起那么多泥。

### 第12页

第四天早晨，阿禾在池边看见自己的影子。

影子不算十分清楚，可眉毛、鼻子都在。它像一个刚睡醒的人，还带着一点迷糊。

### 第13页

老人说：“心也是这样。”

“吵架以后，受委屈以后，太兴奋以后，心里常常都是泥。你若马上判断、马上回嘴、马上做大决定，就像拿竹竿搅池底。”

### 第14页

小石头问：“那是不是只要坐着不动？”

老人说：“不全是。先静，是让泥沉下去；后来还要动，是让水重新流起来。”

### 第15页

他们把清一点的池水引到菜畦。

水不能猛冲，猛冲会把苗根掀起来。老人让他们挖浅沟，让水慢慢走。清水从池塘到菜根，像把安静送出去。

### 第16页

阿禾看着小苗喝水。

他明白“安”不是把自己关起来，“动”也不是到处乱跑。安过以后，再慢慢生出合适的动作，才叫“徐生”。

### 第17页

傍晚，老人拿出一只旧木勺。

勺柄有裂纹，已经用细铜丝缠过。它不新，也不漂亮，可舀水正稳。

### 第18页

“为什么不换新的？”小石头问。

老人说：“它还能用。懂得爱护旧东西，就不必总追新的。人也是，心不要天天求满、求新、求一下子变得了不起。”

## 回扣

### 第19页

“孰能浊以静之徐清”，阿禾在池塘边看见了。

浑水不是骂人的话。水会浑，是因为风雨搅动；心会浑，是因为事情太多、情绪太急。

### 第20页

“孰能安以久动之徐生”，也在菜畦里。

静下来以后，不是永远不动。清一点的水要流向需要它的地方，安下来的心也要去做该做的事。

### 第21页

“保此道者不欲盈”，像那只旧木勺。

不必把自己装成永远崭新、永远完美。裂过的地方能被修好，旧东西也能有长久的用处。

### 第22页

阿禾写下：浑的时候，先别急着证明自己清。

静一静，泥会沉；清一点，再慢慢动。水这样，人心也可以这样。

# Chapter 015-3: Muddy Water Slowly Clears

## Allusion

### Page 1

Chapter Fifteen has a line that belongs beside a cup of water:

"Who can make muddy water clear? Let it be still, and it will gradually become clear."

### Page 2

After a storm, the small pond in Kongkong Village turned yellow with mud.

Broken grass floated on the surface, and silt moved below. Even the white goose, who usually loved looking at her reflection, stood on the bank and refused to go in.

### Page 3

Ahe wanted to help the pond clear faster.

He brought a long pole and stirred the water, trying to lift out the grass. As soon as the pole moved, mud from the bottom rose again, and the water became even muddier.

### Page 4

Little Stone brought a cloth bag. "Let's filter the water."

They scooped a bucket of water and poured it into the bag. Fine mud slowly leaked through the cloth. Hands, shoes, and ground all turned yellow.

### Page 5

After half a day, the pond was not clear, and the children looked as if they had been pulled from mud.

Ahe grew annoyed. "Why does helping make it messier?"

### Page 6

The old man did not laugh at them.

He only filled a bowl with muddy water and set it on the stone step. "Watch this bowl first."

### Page 7

At first, everything in the water moved.

Mud dots were like tiny clouds, and bits of grass floated up and down. Ahe wanted to touch it, but the old man shook his head. "Do not hurry."

### Page 8

After a while, the heavier mud slowly sank.

The top cleared a little first, then the middle brightened. A thin layer of yellow silt gathered at the bottom.

### Page 9

Ahe whispered, "It can clear by itself."

The old man said, "Not that we do nothing. First, do not manage it wildly. When things are muddy, hurried hands are most dangerous."

### Page 10

They cleared the pond's inlet so new rainwater could slowly flow through.

They also removed broken branches blown in by the wind. They did only what was needed and stopped pushing poles into the pond bottom.

### Page 11

The next day, the pond was still yellow, but stones could be seen in the shallows.

On the third day, the white goose tried the water. Her webbed feet made ripples, but did not stir up much mud.

### Page 12

On the fourth morning, Ahe saw his reflection by the pond.

It was not perfectly clear, but eyebrows and nose were there. It looked like someone just waking up, still a little hazy.

### Page 13

The old man said, "The heart is like this too."

"After an argument, after hurt feelings, after too much excitement, the heart is often muddy. If you judge at once, answer back at once, or make a large decision at once, it is like stirring the pond bottom."

### Page 14

Little Stone asked, "Then should we only sit and never move?"

The old man said, "Not exactly. First stillness lets the mud settle. Later, movement lets the water live again."

### Page 15

They guided some clearer pond water toward the vegetable patch.

The water could not rush, or it would tear up the roots. The old man had them dig shallow channels so the water could move slowly. Clear water traveled from pond to vegetables, as if sending quiet outward.

### Page 16

Ahe watched the seedlings drink.

He understood that rest is not locking oneself away, and movement is not running everywhere. After rest, the right action slowly grows. That is gradual life.

### Page 17

At dusk, the old man took out an old wooden ladle.

Its handle had a crack bound with thin copper wire. It was not new or pretty, but it scooped water steadily.

### Page 18

"Why not get a new one?" Little Stone asked.

The old man said, "It can still be used. If we know how to care for old things, we do not need to chase the new all the time. People are similar. The heart need not always seek fullness, newness, or sudden greatness."

## Return

### Page 19

Ahe saw "let muddy water be still and it will gradually become clear" beside the pond.

Muddy water is not an insult. Water becomes muddy because wind and rain stir it. The heart becomes muddy because too many matters and rushed feelings stir it.

### Page 20

"Let rest and movement gradually arise" was also in the vegetable patch.

After becoming still, one does not remain unmoving forever. Clearer water should flow where it is needed, and a settled heart should do what needs doing.

### Page 21

"Not wishing to be full" is like the old wooden ladle.

We do not have to pretend to be new and perfect all the time. Cracked places can be repaired, and old things can keep their long usefulness.

### Page 22

Ahe wrote: when muddy, do not hurry to prove you are clear.

Be still, and silt will sink. When there is some clarity, move slowly. Water can do this, and so can the human heart.
