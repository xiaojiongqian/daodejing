# 第009-1章：运夷——功遂身退

## 原文

### 第1页

持而盈之，不如其已；揣而棁之，不可长保。

白话：端持着器皿还要把它添得满满的，不如适时停下；一件器物已经尖利，还要反复锻打磨削，它的锋芒便难以长久保存。

### 第2页

金玉满堂，莫之能守；富贵而骄，自遗其咎。

白话：金玉堆满厅堂，没有谁能够永远守住；拥有财富与地位以后若变得骄纵，便是在为自己留下祸患。

### 第3页

功遂身退，天之道。

白话：事情完成以后，不再长久占据功劳与位置，懂得退让和移交，这合乎天地运行的规律。

河上公把本章题为“运夷”。这个题目可以帮助我们想到变化中的平衡，却不必把它强解成唯一确定的词义。本章真正关心的是：一件事已经到达高处以后，人能不能停止原来的动作。

## 释义

### 第4页

“持”本来已经是一种守住。王弼由此指出：一个人既已持守所得，却仍想继续增益，危险便从“还要更多”开始。老子并不反对努力，也没有把未满当作美德。他所警惕的，是完成以后仍不肯停手。

河上公把“盈”解释为满，把“已”解释为止。杯子的问题不在于装了水，而在于水已经够用，手却仍在倾倒。许多损失不是来自缺少能力，而是来自能力没有听见“够了”。

### 第5页

“揣而棁之”在不同传本中写法不一。王弼本作“棁”，河上公本作读者更熟悉的“锐”。两种文字共同指向一个动作：器物已经形成尖端，人还要把它治得更尖、更利。

锋利当然有用。问题是，若把“更锐”当成唯一尺度，器物会变薄，使用者也失去回旋。这个譬喻不能被夸大成“凡事到极端必然反转”的定律；它只是让我们看见，增益有时会跨过界线，开始伤害原本想保存的东西。

### 第6页

“金玉满堂”谈的是占有的限度，“富贵而骄”谈的是占有之后的心态。财富并不是罪，也不会神秘地自动招来灾祸。但财富越多，需要保管、判断和分配的事情也越多；时间与变故仍可能改变所有权。

真正危险的是“骄”。处境优越以后，人容易把时运、协助和制度都误认成个人才能，于是越来越听不见异议。所谓“自遗其咎”，往往不是天外飞来的惩罚，而是现实被长期拒绝以后，终于以代价的形式返回。

### 第7页

**运夷：** 河上公为第九章所加的章题。可借以理解运行中的平衡，但不宜据此虚构一套确定词源。

**揣、棁与锐：** “揣”在这里接近加工、锻治；王弼本作“棁”，河上公本作“锐”。古注都把重点落在过度求尖利难以久保。

**功遂与功成名遂：** 本篇正文依王弼本作“功遂身退”；河上公本作“功成、名遂、身退”。两者都把退放在完成之后。

**身退：** 不只指辞官或隐居，也可以是让位、移交、停止独占功名。王弼以四时递换解释它：一季完成自己的工作，便让下一季到来。

## 画面描述

### 第8页

夜深的工坊里，炉火已经暗了。年轻匠人把一只盛满清水的铜杯放在案上，又提起水壶；随后，他拿起一柄已经开刃的小刀，准备再磨一遍。老师傅没有阻止，只问：“你是在完成它，还是舍不得结束？”

年轻人停住手。杯沿已有水珠落下，磨石上的刀声也显得过于轻薄。

“可我还能把它做得更好。”他说。

“也许。”老人答道，“但‘还能做’，和‘还应该做’，不是同一句话。”

他把水壶放回原处，又将工坊的钥匙交给年轻人：“最后一门手艺，是知道什么时候停手。最后一次交接，是让作品不必永远依靠你。”

## 正文

### 第9页

现代生活很少奖励停止。软件发布以后，还要添加更多功能；一份报告已经回答了问题，还要塞入更多图表；一场晚宴本来足够愉快，主人却担心不够丰盛。

于是，“更多”悄悄取代了“为何”。功能越多，产品未必越好用；信息越多，判断未必越清楚。满的危险不在拥有，而在新增之物已经不能服务目的，我们却仍把添加误认为认真。

### 第10页

“锐”也会出现在人的语言里。一个聪明人很容易发现别人的漏洞，也很容易用一句准确的话赢得争论。但如果每一次谈话都要证明自己更敏锐，亲近的人便不再说出尚未成熟的想法。

锋芒不是罪。医生需要准确，工程师需要严谨，管理者也必须指出问题。老子提醒的只是：真正长久的能力，还包括收锋。若一种才华只能压倒别人，它最终会失去合作、修复与学习的空间。

### 第11页

我们常把拥有想成故事的终点：得到房子、职位、收藏或声望，便以为不安会随之结束。但每一份拥有也带来新的工作。房屋要维护，职位要负责，名声要回应，资产要判断。

“莫之能守”不是劝人拒绝财物，而是取消一种幻觉：所有权不能使事物脱离时间。明白这一点，人反而能更从容地使用财富，不必把全部生命都变成一场防守。

### 第12页

富贵最隐蔽的风险，不是奢华，而是反馈变得昂贵。职位越高，越少有人愿意说“这个决定错了”；成就越多，越容易把反对听成冒犯。

因此，谦逊并非礼貌性的自我贬低，而是一种接收现实的能力。一个领导者主动询问坏消息，一个专业人士允许新人纠正自己，都是在减少“骄”留下的盲区。能听见不舒服的话，往往比说出漂亮的话更能守住成果。

### 第13页

“功遂身退”最容易被理解成事业一成便立刻离开。它当然可能包括离职或归隐，却不只如此。许多时候，退只是改变自己与成果的关系。

创办者可以从亲自决定一切，转为培养接替者；父母可以从替孩子安排道路，转为让他承担选择；作者完成作品后，也要允许读者产生不受控制的理解。退不是抹去贡献，而是不再要求贡献永远证明自己的重要。

### 第14页

《史记》记载，张良在汉初论功行封时，没有接受任择齐地三万户的厚赏，只请求受封于“留”——那是他早年与刘邦相遇之地。后来，他也表达过退意，并转向清静自守。

张良的一生并不能被整理成毫无矛盾的隐士传奇，他也并非从政治中彻底消失。这个故事可贵之处，正在于它没有要求人否认功劳，而是提出一个分寸：功劳已经得到承认以后，是否还要把它不断兑换成更多占有？

### 第15页

斯多葛哲学也提醒人们，财富、名誉与结果并不完全受我们控制。它与老子相遇之处，是不让外在成果成为内心唯一的支柱。若幸福只能靠下一次胜利来续期，我们便从未真正拥有胜利。

但两者的重心并不相同。斯多葛学派更强调判断与德性，老子在本章则让我们听见时势的节律：器满则止，功成则移，如四时各尽其事，又不阻拦下一季。

## 回扣

### 第16页

本章的五句，一步步从器物走向人心。

器皿已经满了，便不要再加；锋刃已经利了，便不要只求更尖；金玉再多，也不能逃离时间；富贵若使人拒绝现实，过失便从内部生长；事情完成以后，则要为后来者和下一阶段让出位置。

老子反对的不是丰盛、才能、财富或成功。他反对的是把它们变成没有终点的动作。

### 第17页

真正的完成，不是把最后一点空间也占满。

它是水到杯沿时，手能够停下；一句话足够时，不再补上伤人的最后一句；团队已经能够前行时，领导者不必继续站在每个决定中央。

知止并不会缩小生命。它保存锋芒，也保存余地。功遂而退，不是把成功丢下，而是让成功终于有机会离开我们的掌心，走向它自己的后来。

# Chapter 009-1: Keeping to the Level—Stepping Back When the Work Is Done

## Original Text

### Page 1

Hold something and keep filling it: better to stop. Work a point and keep sharpening it: its edge cannot long endure.

In plain language: when a vessel is already full, continued pouring does not improve it. When a point is already sharp, repeated working may make its sharpness impossible to preserve.

### Page 2

Gold and jade may fill the hall, yet no one can guard them forever. Wealth and rank joined with arrogance leave trouble in their own wake.

In plain language: possessions cannot be made permanent. When privilege hardens into pride, a person begins creating the conditions of their own harm.

### Page 3

When the work is fulfilled, step back. This is the way of Heaven.

In plain language: once a task has been completed, do not occupy its credit and position forever. Make room, hand over, and let the next movement begin.

Heshang Gong titles this chapter “Keeping to the Level.” The title may suggest balance within change, though it should not be forced into a single certain etymology. The chapter's central question is simpler: once something has reached its height, can we stop doing what brought it there?

## Interpretation

### Page 4

To “hold” already means that something has been secured. Wang Bi therefore hears a warning: after gaining and preserving something, we still demand increase. The danger begins with “still more.” Laozi is not against effort, nor does he make incompleteness a virtue. He questions effort that cannot recognize completion.

Heshang Gong glosses fullness as being filled and “enough” as stopping. The cup is not at fault for containing water. The trouble begins when there is already enough and the hand continues to pour. Many losses come not from a lack of ability, but from an ability that cannot hear the word enough.

### Page 5

The line about working a point survives in different forms. Wang Bi's text uses a character suggesting a shaped point; Heshang Gong's has the more familiar word “sharp.” Both versions picture an object already brought to an edge and then worked sharper still.

Sharpness has obvious uses. Yet when “sharper” becomes the only measure, the object grows thin and the user loses room to adjust. This image should not be inflated into a law that every extreme must reverse. It shows something more modest and useful: addition may cross a threshold and begin damaging what it meant to preserve.

### Page 6

“Gold and jade filling the hall” concerns the limits of possession. “Wealth and rank joined with arrogance” concerns the mind after possession. Wealth is not a sin, nor does it mysteriously summon disaster. But the more one owns, the more one must maintain, judge, and distribute. Time and circumstance remain beyond ownership.

The deeper danger is pride. Advantage can tempt us to mistake timing, assistance, and institutions for personal brilliance. Dissent then becomes harder to hear. The trouble we “leave for ourselves” is often no supernatural punishment. It is reality returning as a cost after being refused for too long.

### Page 7

**Keeping to the Level:** Heshang Gong's title for Chapter Nine. It may guide us toward balance in movement, but does not justify an invented, definitive origin.

**Working, shaping, and sharpening:** The received versions differ, but the old commentaries share a concern with an edge worked beyond durability.

**Work fulfilled and work-and-name accomplished:** This chapter follows Wang Bi's shorter wording. Heshang Gong's version reads, “work accomplished, name achieved, step back.” Both place withdrawal after completion.

**Stepping back:** It may mean retirement, but also yielding place, transferring authority, or ceasing to monopolize credit. Wang Bi compares it to the seasons: one completes its work and allows another to arrive.

## Visual Description

### Page 8

Late at night, the fire in a workshop has dimmed. A young craftsperson sets a bronze cup filled with clear water on the bench and raises the pitcher again. Then he takes up an already sharpened knife and prepares to return it to the stone. The old master does not stop him. He merely asks, “Are you finishing it, or are you unable to finish?”

The young man pauses. A bead of water has crossed the rim. The sound of the blade against the stone has become perilously thin.

“But I can still make it better,” he says.

“Perhaps,” the elder replies. “But ‘I can continue’ and ‘I should continue’ are not the same sentence.”

He puts down the pitcher and gives the young man the workshop key. “The last craft is knowing when to stop. The last handover is letting the work live without you.”

## Main Text

### Page 9

Modern life rarely rewards stopping. After software is released, more features must be added. A report has answered its question, yet more charts are inserted. A dinner is already generous, yet the host fears it is not generous enough.

More quietly replaces why. A product with more functions may become harder to use; a decision with more information may become less clear. The danger of fullness is not possession. It is the moment when addition no longer serves the purpose, while we continue to mistake addition for care.

### Page 10

Sharpness appears in speech as well. An intelligent person quickly sees the weakness in another's argument and may win with one precise sentence. But if every conversation must display superior acuity, those nearby stop offering thoughts that are not yet complete.

An edge is not a vice. A doctor needs precision, an engineer needs rigor, and a manager must name problems. Laozi's caution is narrower: lasting ability includes the ability to sheathe itself. Talent that can only defeat will eventually lose the space required for cooperation, repair, and learning.

### Page 11

We often imagine possession as the end of a story. Once we obtain the home, title, collection, or reputation, unease is supposed to end. Yet every possession creates work. Houses need repair, offices carry duties, reputations demand responses, and assets require judgment.

“No one can guard them forever” does not tell us to reject property. It removes an illusion: ownership cannot take anything outside time. Once we understand this, we can use wealth with greater ease, without turning an entire life into an act of defence.

### Page 12

The most concealed risk of privilege is not luxury but the rising price of honest feedback. The higher the office, the fewer people willing to say, “This decision is wrong.” The longer the record of success, the easier it is to hear objection as insult.

Humility, then, is not ceremonial self-belittlement. It is the ability to receive reality. A leader who asks for bad news, or an expert who lets a novice correct them, reduces the blind spot left by pride. Hearing an uncomfortable truth often preserves an achievement better than speaking an impressive one.

### Page 13

“Step back when the work is done” is easily heard as an order to leave a career the instant success arrives. It may indeed include resignation or retreat, but it is not limited to them. Often, stepping back means changing one's relationship to the result.

A founder can move from deciding everything to preparing a successor. Parents can move from arranging a child's path to letting the child bear a choice. An author, after finishing a book, must permit readers to form meanings beyond control. To step back is not to erase one's contribution. It is to stop requiring the contribution to prove one's importance forever.

### Page 14

The Records of the Grand Historian tells us that when rewards were assigned after the founding of the Han, Zhang Liang did not accept the offer to choose thirty thousand households in Qi. He asked only to be enfeoffed at Liu, the place associated with his early meeting with Liu Bang. Later, he also spoke of withdrawing toward a quieter life.

His life should not be polished into a contradiction-free legend of renunciation; he did not simply vanish from political affairs. Its value lies in a more difficult measure. Once service has been acknowledged, must every achievement be converted into still more possession?

### Page 15

Stoic philosophy likewise reminds us that wealth, fame, and outcomes do not lie wholly within our control. It meets Laozi in refusing to make external success the sole foundation of inner life. If happiness must be renewed by the next victory, we have never truly possessed the present one.

The emphases remain different. Stoicism attends chiefly to judgment and virtue. Laozi asks us to hear the rhythm of circumstance: stop when the vessel is full, move when the work is done, as each season completes its task without preventing the next.

## Return

### Page 16

The chapter's five movements travel from objects into the human heart.

When a vessel is full, do not keep adding. When an edge is sharp, do not make sharpness the only goal. No accumulation of gold escapes time. When privilege refuses reality, trouble grows from within. When the work is complete, leave space for the next person and the next phase.

Laozi is not against abundance, talent, wealth, or success. He is against turning them into actions with no ending.

### Page 17

True completion does not occupy the last remaining space.

It is the hand that stops when water reaches the rim; the restraint that leaves an unnecessary final sentence unsaid; the leader who no longer needs to stand at the centre of every decision once the team can move.

Knowing when to stop does not make life smaller. It preserves the edge and the open space around it. To step back is not to abandon success. It is to let success leave our grasp and acquire a future of its own.
