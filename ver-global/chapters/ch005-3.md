# 第005-3章：不仁与无我

## 原文

### 第1页

天地不仁，以万物为刍狗；圣人不仁，以百姓为刍狗。天地之间，其犹橐籥乎？虚而不屈，动而愈出。多言数穷，不如守中。

### 第2页

天地不仁，并非指自然冷酷，而是说天地不以偏私和情感来对待万物；圣人不仁，则是说治理者不以私爱来干预公共秩序。真正的无为，不是消灭情感，而是让情感不侵入制度。

## 释义

### 第3页

这一章把“仁”从私人情感放到公共秩序中重新审视。老子并不否定人有怜爱之心，但他反对治理者借仁爱之名施舍、偏袒，最终仍让一切围绕自己运转。

### 第4页

“天地不仁”所指的，不是冷酷，而是超越偏私。万物各自生长，各自成其所成，天地并不因为某一物而偏爱或排斥。圣人效法这一点，治理时不以私人好恶取代公共规则。

## 术语与考据简注

### 第5页

“刍狗”是古代祭礼中供祭的草扎狗，祭毕即弃，象征物件在仪式中的作用是暂时的，不应执著。

“橐籥”指古代风箱，空而不屈，鼓动后气息愈出，说明虚能生动。

“守中”是保持内心的平衡，不被外物和多言牵引。

## 正文

### 第6页

我们可以把“天地不仁”分成四个层次来理解。首先，它不是人格神的审判视角。天地不是一个会奖善罚恶、偏爱某些对象的主宰。它只是容纳万物、让万物各自生长的背景。

### 第7页

其次，“不仁”不是对人的否定，而是对偏私的否定。若统治者以仁爱之名来偏袒某一方，便会把公共秩序变成私人情感的附属物。老子要反对的，恰恰是这种把爱变成控制的做法。

### 第8页

再次，“不仁”不是让人麻木，而是让人不执著于自我。真正的“无我”，并不是抹去自我，而是使自我不再把自己的好恶当成宇宙的中心。这样的人，既保有个体性，也不会把主观偏见强加于他人。

### 第9页

最后，“橐籥”与“守中”说明，真正的力量不在于充满，而在于空明。一个人若心里装满了欲望、执念与多言，就会自我耗尽；只有守住中道，才能不被外界牵引而失去本真。

## 回扣

### 第10页

这一章不是教人冷酷。它要求我们分清怜爱与支配：爱可以进入人与人的关系，却不应成为治理者随意改变公共尺度的理由。这样，个人不必压抑情感，社会也不必服从某个人的亲疏好恶。

# Chapter 005-3: Non-Benevolence and Selflessness

## Original Text

### Page 1

Heaven and earth do not act from (the impulse of) any wish to be benevolent; they deal with all things as the dogs of grass are dealt with. The sages do not act from (any wish to be) benevolent; they deal with the people as the dogs of grass are dealt with.

### Page 2

May not the space between heaven and earth be compared to a bellows?

'Tis emptied, yet it loses not its power;
'Tis moved again, and sends forth air the more.
Much speech to swift exhaustion lead we see;
Your inner being guard, and keep it free.

## Interpretation

### Page 3

This chapter reconsiders benevolence by moving from private feeling to public order. Laozi does not deny human affection. He objects when rulers invoke affection to dispense favors, show partiality, and make the social world revolve around themselves.

### Page 4

When Laozi says that heaven and earth are “not benevolent,” he means that they are not ruled by partiality. All things grow and complete themselves in their own way; heaven and earth do not favor one thing over another. The sage governs by following this example and refusing to replace public order with private preference.

## Key Terms and Notes

### Page 5

“Straw dogs” were ritual objects used in sacrifice and then discarded, suggesting that their function is temporary rather than something to be clung to.

“A bellows” is an image of emptiness and movement, showing that power comes from vacancy and openness rather than fullness.

“Guard the inner being” means keeping the mind centered and not being pulled off course by external noise and excess speech.

## Main Text

### Page 6

We can understand “heaven and earth are not benevolent” in four layers. First, it is not the perspective of a personal deity who judges and rewards. Heaven and earth are not a ruler who favors one side and punishes another. They are the background that allows all things to arise and develop.

### Page 7

Second, “non-benevolence” is not a denial of humanity; it is a rejection of partiality. If a ruler uses the language of benevolence to favor one group, public order becomes an extension of private feeling. What Laozi opposes is exactly this transformation of love into domination.

### Page 8

Third, “non-benevolence” does not make a person numb. It frees a person from attachment to the self. True selflessness is not the erasure of the self, but the refusal to treat one’s own likes and dislikes as the center of the world. In this way one preserves individuality without imposing subjective bias on others.

### Page 9

Finally, the images of the bellows and the inner center show that true power lies not in fullness but in emptiness. If the mind is crowded with desire, obsession, and excessive talk, it becomes exhausted. Only by keeping the middle can one remain rooted and uncarried away by the world.

## Return to the Original

### Page 10

The chapter does not teach coldness. It asks us to distinguish care from control. Affection belongs in human relationships, but it should not license a ruler to alter the public measure at will. People need not suppress feeling, and society need not submit to one person’s preferences.
