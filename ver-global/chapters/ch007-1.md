# 第007-1章：韬光而长久

## 原文

### 第1页

天长地久。天地所以能长且久者，以其不自生，故能长生。

白话：天地之所以能够长久，是因为它们不只为自己的存在而忙碌，不同万物争夺生机；正因不以自己为唯一目的，反而得以长久运行。

### 第2页

是以圣人后其身而身先；外其身而身存。

白话：所以有道的人不急着把自己放在最前面，反而受到众人的推重；不把个人得失看成一切，生命与事业反而更能得到保全。

### 第3页

非以其无私邪，故能成其私。

白话：不正因为他没有处处盘算私利，最终才成全了自身吗？这里的“成其私”不是精明的回报设计，而是说：当一个人不再紧抓自己，许多真正属于他的东西才有机会留下。

## 释义

### 第4页

“不自生”容易被读成“天地不是自己生出来的”，但王弼的注释把重点放在另一处：若只求自己的生存，便会同万物争；不以自身为中心，万物反而愿意归附。河上公也说，天地安静地施予而不求回报，不像人总急着为自己多取一点。

天地当然没有人的道德意图。老子借它们的长久提醒我们：一个系统若只顾维持自己，往往会耗尽使它存在的环境；一个人若时时证明自己重要，也可能破坏那份真正的信任。

### 第5页

“后其身”不是叫人永远退让，“外其身”也不是轻视身体、取消边界。老子反对的，是让自我成为所有事情的唯一尺度：每句话都要赢，每件事都要署名，每段关系都要证明自己不可替代。

把自己放在后面，有时只是少说最后一句，让更熟悉问题的人先发言；有时是在功劳簿上写下团队的名字；有时则是在危险真正来临时，承担一个职位本来就该承担的责任。

### 第6页

“故能成其私”是本章最微妙的转折。它不是一条保证“先付出就一定有回报”的成功公式。许多无私的人未必被看见，许多慷慨的行为也不会得到补偿。

老子谈的更像一种长期的因果：当人不再把每次行动都变成自我宣传，关系便少了一层算计；当组织不只追逐眼前利益，信任才可能累积。所谓“成其私”，不是夺回更多，而是终于拥有那些无法靠夺取得到的东西。

## 术语与考据简注

### 第7页

**韬光**：河上公本为第七章所加的章题，可理解为收敛光芒、不以炫耀争先。它并非要求隐藏能力，而是让能力服务于事情本身。

**不自生**：不只为自身生存而经营，不与万物争夺一切。王弼概括为“自生则与物争，不自生则物归”。

**后其身 / 外其身**：把个人名位放在后面，把个人得失暂时置于更大的目标之外；不等于自我抹除或容忍伤害。

**无私 / 成其私**：不以私欲主导行动，反而使自身获得较为长久的安顿。这是一种悖论性的结果，不是算计回报的技巧。

## 正文

### 第8页

**画面描述**：雪线下的清晨，一间衣物修补工坊刚刚亮灯。长木桌上摊着一件磨白、开线的旧外套，窗外是被晨光慢慢照亮的山脊。年轻同事翻看订单，轻声问：“一件衣服穿得越久，我们不是卖得越少吗？”老裁缝没有抬头，只把针穿过旧布：“也许少一次买卖，却多一分信任。”镜头沿着细密的针脚向前移动：线把裂口合拢，桌边放着一张不醒目的纸条——“修好它，不必换掉它。”工坊里没有英雄式的聚光灯，只有几双手依次把衣服传下去。

### 第9页

我们生活在一个十分擅长把“在前面”变成美德的时代。职位要领先，观点要抢先，消息要最先发布；即使在休息时，也要把风景拍下来，向别人证明自己正在过一种值得羡慕的生活。

问题不在于雄心。雄心可以建造医院、公司和作品。问题在于，当自我形象坐上每个决定的主席位，工作便不再只需完成，还必须反复证明“这是我的”；关系也不再只是相爱，还要不断确认“我是否最重要”。

### 第10页

现代职场里，最稀缺的领导力往往不是第一个给出答案，而是创造一个别人能够给出答案的空间。一个负责人可以最后发言，却最先承担后果；可以让同事站到台前，却不把责任一并推走。

这就是“后其身”与消极退缩的区别。退缩的人离开了事情，韬光的人仍在事情深处。他不抢夺可见度，却守住方向、边界与承诺，因此团队记住的不是他的音量，而是有他在场时，大家能够把事情做得更好。

### 第11页

参考资料中收录的 Patagonia 案例，把这个古老悖论带入现代商业。公司从制作耐用的攀岩工具和衣物起步，成长之后仍把产品耐用与环境保护置于品牌中心；自1985年起，它承诺把销售额的百分之一用于环境保护，并参与推动“1% for the Planet”。

2022年，创始人伊冯·乔伊纳德一家没有通过出售或上市将公司套现，而是把所有权转入 Patagonia Purpose Trust 与 Holdfast Collective。企业继续营利运营，但除再投入公司之外的利润，将用于自然保护和应对环境危机。

### 第12页

这个故事动人的地方，不在于它证明“善良一定更赚钱”。若把无私当成品牌策略，无私便立刻成了另一种私心。它真正提出的问题是：一家企业究竟把什么视为目的，把什么视为工具？

若利润是唯一目的，土地、员工、顾客乃至未来都容易沦为消耗品；若企业承认自己依赖一个更大的共同世界，利润便可以成为维护这个世界的工具。把地球放在前面，并没有让公司消失；它反而为公司的存在给出了一个超越季度报表的理由。

### 第13页

中国传统中，范仲淹的《岳阳楼记》提供了另一种回声。他应滕子京之请，为重修岳阳楼作记，从洞庭湖的阴晴变化写到人的荣辱悲欢，最后留下“先天下之忧而忧，后天下之乐而乐”。

这句话与老子并不完全相同。范仲淹强调士大夫承担公共责任，老子则更根本地追问自我应当站在何处。但两者都把个人情绪放进更辽阔的尺度：不是没有自己的忧乐，而是不让自己的忧乐成为世界唯一的天气。

### 第14页

古罗马斯多亚学派也从另一条路逼近这个问题。爱比克泰德区分我们能够掌握的事——判断、意愿与取舍——和我们无法完全掌握的事——身体、财产、名誉与他人的评价。他劝人把力量放回自己的选择，而不是耗在控制外部世界上。

老子与斯多亚并非同一种哲学，但它们在此相互照亮：当一个人不再把名声和位置当成必须守住的城墙，他才更自由地去做眼前真正有益的事。“外其身”并不是抛弃自己，而是不再把公众眼中的自己当作全部的自己。

### 第15页

放在家庭里，这一道理更为细微。父母若把孩子的每项成就都当作自己的成绩，爱便容易变成控制；伴侣若把每次分歧都当作地位之争，再亲密的对话也会变成法庭。把自己放在后面，有时只是先问一句：“你现在需要我回答，还是需要我听完？”

真正的无私不要求一个人没有需要。它要求我们承认：自己的需要是真实的，却不是房间里唯一真实的东西。一个能为他人留出位置的人，并没有因此缩小；他只是终于不必占满整个世界，才能确信自己存在。

## 回扣

### 第16页

“天长地久”不是对永恒的浪漫赞美，而是一则关于长久条件的观察：凡是只为维持自身而不断攫取的，终会损伤使它得以存在的关系；凡是能让生命在其中共同展开的，反而较可能延续。

所以“后其身而身先”不是名位上的花招，“外其身而身存”也不是牺牲式的自我感动。它们描述的是一种重心的移动——从“怎样让我显得重要”，移向“什么值得被我认真完成”。

### 第17页

一棵树并不在树荫上写下自己的名字，天空也不向被照亮的人索要感谢。它们只是各自成其所是，于是许多生命得以在其中停留。

人或许无法像天地那样无心，却可以少一点把自己推到画面中央的急切。奇妙的是，当我们退后半步，世界并没有忘记我们；它第一次有空间，看清我们真正给予了什么。

# Chapter 007-1: Enduring by Stepping Back

## Original Text

### Page 1

Heaven is long-enduring and earth continues long. The reason why heaven and earth are able to endure and continue thus long is because they do not live of, or for, themselves. This is how they are able to continue and endure.

In modern terms: heaven and earth endure because they do not treat their own continuance as their only purpose. They do not compete with the ten thousand things for life, and so their larger pattern can go on.

### Page 2

Therefore the sage puts his own person last, and yet it is found in the foremost place; he treats his person as if it were foreign to him, and yet that person is preserved.

In modern terms: the sage does not hurry to stand at the front, yet people come to trust his leadership. He does not make personal gain the measure of everything, and so his life and work are more likely to be preserved.

### Page 3

Is it not because he has no personal and private ends, that therefore such ends are realised?

In modern terms: precisely because he is not forever arranging events for his own advantage, what is genuinely his may finally endure. This is not a clever bargain with the universe. It is an outcome that appears when the self loosens its grip.

## Interpretation

### Page 4

“It does not live for itself” can sound as though Laozi were making a claim about who created heaven and earth. Wang Bi’s commentary turns our attention elsewhere: whatever lives only for itself must contend with other things; what does not make itself the centre allows things to gather around it. Heshang Gong likewise describes heaven and earth as steady, giving without demanding repayment.

Heaven and earth, of course, do not possess human moral intentions. Laozi uses their endurance to expose a human paradox: a system concerned only with preserving itself may destroy the conditions on which it depends; a person forever proving their importance may damage the trust that would make them truly important.

### Page 5

“To put oneself last” does not mean perpetual surrender, and “to treat oneself as outside” does not mean despising the body or abandoning boundaries. Laozi is challenging the habit of making the self the sole measure of every event: every conversation must be won, every achievement signed, every relationship made to prove our indispensability.

Sometimes stepping back means letting the person closest to the problem speak first. Sometimes it means placing the team’s names on the work. And sometimes, when real danger arrives, it means accepting the responsibility that a position was created to bear.

### Page 6

“And so he fulfils his own ends” is the chapter’s most delicate turn. It does not promise that generosity will always be rewarded. Many selfless people remain unseen, and many generous acts receive no compensation.

Laozi is describing a slower kind of causality. When every action is no longer a piece of self-advertisement, relationships contain less calculation. When an institution is not governed by immediate gain alone, trust has time to accumulate. To “fulfil one’s own ends” is not to seize more, but to possess at last what seizure could never secure.

## Key Terms and Notes

### Page 7

**Concealing the light**: Heshang Gong’s title for Chapter Seven. It suggests restraining one’s brilliance rather than competing through display. It does not ask us to hide ability, but to let ability serve the work.

**Not living for itself**: Not organising existence solely around self-preservation or contending with everything else. Wang Bi writes, in effect: living for oneself produces rivalry; not living for oneself allows things to return.

**Putting oneself last / treating oneself as outside**: Placing rank and private gain behind a larger purpose. Neither phrase requires self-erasure or the tolerance of harm.

**Having no private ends / fulfilling one’s own ends**: When private appetite does not govern action, the self may find a more durable place. This is a paradoxical result, not a technique for calculating returns.

## Main Text

### Page 8

**Visual description**: Morning light reaches a repair workshop beneath a line of snow-covered mountains. A worn jacket lies open on a long wooden table, its faded cloth split at one seam. A younger colleague studies the order sheet and asks, “If clothes last longer, do we not sell fewer of them?” Without looking up, the older tailor draws a needle through the cloth. “Perhaps we lose one sale,” she says, “and gain a little trust.” The camera follows the close, even stitches as they draw the tear together. At the edge of the table sits a modest note: “Repair it. Do not replace it.” No one stands in a heroic spotlight; several pairs of hands simply pass the garment onward.

### Page 9

We live in an age unusually skilled at turning “being first” into a virtue. Our position should lead, our opinion should arrive earliest, our news should be posted first. Even at rest, we photograph the view in order to prove that our life is enviable.

Ambition is not the problem. Ambition can build hospitals, companies, and works of art. The trouble begins when the public image of the self takes the chair at every decision. Work must then do more than succeed: it must repeatedly announce, “This is mine.” Love must do more than care: it must continually reassure us, “I matter most.”

### Page 10

In modern organisations, the rarest kind of leadership is often not the ability to answer first, but the ability to create a space in which others can answer. A leader may speak last while taking responsibility first. They may place colleagues in the foreground without pushing accountability out of sight.

This is the difference between stepping back and withdrawing. The person who withdraws leaves the work; the person who conceals the light remains deep inside it. They do not compete for visibility, yet they hold direction, boundaries, and promises steady. The team remembers not the volume of their voice, but that in their presence everyone could do better work.

### Page 11

The Patagonia case preserved in the project’s reference materials carries this ancient paradox into modern business. Beginning with durable tools and clothing for climbers, the company later placed product longevity and environmental protection at the centre of its public mission. Since 1985, it has committed one per cent of sales to environmental protection and helped advance 1% for the Planet.

In 2022, founder Yvon Chouinard and his family did not sell the company or take it public for their own payout. They transferred ownership to the Patagonia Purpose Trust and the Holdfast Collective. The business remained a for-profit company, while profits not reinvested in the company were directed toward protecting nature and responding to environmental crisis.

### Page 12

What moves us here is not proof that virtue always makes more money. The moment selflessness becomes a branding formula, it becomes another form of self-interest. The deeper question is this: what does a company treat as an end, and what does it treat as a tool?

If profit is the only end, land, employees, customers, and the future all risk becoming consumable means. If a company recognises its dependence on a larger shared world, profit can become a means of protecting that world. Placing the earth first did not make the company disappear. It gave the company a reason to exist beyond the quarter’s report.

### Page 13

Chinese tradition offers another echo in Fan Zhongyan’s “Record of Yueyang Tower.” Writing at the request of his friend Teng Zijing after the tower was restored, Fan moves from the changing weather of Dongting Lake to the changing fortunes of human life. He ends with the resolve to be “the first to worry over the troubles of the world, and the last to enjoy its pleasures.”

Fan’s line is not identical to Laozi’s. Fan speaks of the public duty of the scholar-official; Laozi asks more fundamentally where the self should stand. Yet both place personal mood inside a wider horizon. One still has private sorrow and joy, but they no longer become the world’s only weather.

### Page 14

The ancient Stoics approached the question by another road. Epictetus distinguished what lies within our power—judgment, intention, and choice—from what cannot be fully commanded—body, property, reputation, and the opinions of others. He urged his students to return their effort to choice rather than exhaust themselves controlling the world outside.

Laozi and Stoicism are not the same philosophy, but here they cast light on one another. When reputation and position cease to be walls we must defend at any cost, we become freer to do what is actually useful. To “treat oneself as outside” is not to abandon the self; it is to stop mistaking the self seen by the public for the whole of who we are.

### Page 15

At home, the lesson becomes quieter. When parents turn every achievement of a child into evidence of their own success, love easily becomes control. When partners treat every disagreement as a contest for rank, intimacy becomes a courtroom. Stepping back may begin with one question: “Do you need me to answer, or do you need me to listen until you are finished?”

Selflessness does not require a person to have no needs. It asks us to admit that our needs are real but are not the only reality in the room. A person who makes room for others has not grown smaller. They have simply stopped filling the whole world in order to be certain that they exist.

## Return to the Original

### Page 16

“Heaven is long-enduring and earth continues long” is not merely a romantic praise of eternity. It is an observation about the conditions of endurance. Whatever takes endlessly in order to preserve itself will eventually injure the relationships on which it depends. What allows life to unfold alongside it has a better chance of lasting.

“To put oneself last” is therefore not a trick for gaining rank, and “to treat oneself as outside” is not theatrical sacrifice. Both describe a shift in the centre of gravity: away from “How can I appear important?” and toward “What is worth doing well?”

### Page 17

A tree does not carve its name into its shade, and the sky does not ask to be thanked by those it illuminates. Each simply becomes what it is, and many lives find room beneath it.

We cannot be as unselfconscious as heaven and earth. But we can feel less urgency to push ourselves into the centre of the frame. Strangely, when we step back half a pace, the world does not forget us. It has room, perhaps for the first time, to see what we have truly given.
