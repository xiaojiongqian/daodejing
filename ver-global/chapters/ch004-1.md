# 第004-1章：道冲而用之

## 原文

### 第1页

道冲而用之或不盈，渊乎似万物之宗。

挫其锐，解其纷，和其光，同其尘，湛兮似若存。

吾不知谁之子，象帝之先。

### 第2页

道体是虚空的，但其作用无穷。它磨去锋芒，化解纷扰，调和光辉，与尘同流，幽隐而常存。它似乎先于“帝”，不能由一个确定的名字加以穷尽。

## 释义

### 第3页

“道冲而用之”说的是，道本身是虚而不滞的，故能生成、容纳与成全万物；“或不盈”则说明道不以盈满自居，唯其不满，才能常新。

### 第4页

“挫其锐，解其纷，和其光，同其尘”说明道以柔和空明之德，消解极端与冲突，使万物归于平衡。所谓“湛兮似若存”，是道的静默常在；“象帝之先”则把道安置在“帝”的观念之前，而不是把它说成另一位发号施令的神。

## 术语与考据简注

### 第5页

**道冲**：道的本体是空，非死寂之空，而是能容纳、生成、流通万物的动态空性。

**或不盈**：道不自满，不停留于固定形态，故能持续发挥作用。

**和光同尘**：让光辉不炫耀，让存在不显赫，回归平凡与共生。

## 正文

### 第6页

这一章把道的本体与作用并说。它不是一个可被占有的对象，而是万物得以生成和显现的空处。修行者不必炫耀自己的“高明”，而要在日常中学习“挫锐”“解纷”“和光”“同尘”。

## 回扣

### 第7页

本章强调的，不是一种神秘主义的虚空，而是一种能容纳万物、消解矛盾、使生命回归平衡的根本法则。道之所以能成为万物之宗，不在于它以自身为中心，而在于它始终保持空明、不盈不满。

# Chapter 004-1: The Way as Empty Yet Effective

## Original Text

### Page 1

The Tao is (like) the emptiness of a vessel; and in our employment of it we must be on our guard against all fulness. How deep and unfathomable it is, as if it were the Honoured Ancestor of all things!

We should blunt our sharp points, and unravel the complications of things; we should attemper our brightness, and bring ourselves into agreement with the obscurity of others. How pure and still the Tao is, as if it would ever so continue!

I do not know whose son it is. It might appear to have been before God.

### Page 2

Dao is empty in form, yet inexhaustible in use. It blunts sharpness, untangles disorder, tempers brightness, and joins the dust of ordinary life. Hidden yet enduring, it seems to precede even the idea of a sovereign Lord and cannot be exhausted by a fixed name.

## Interpretation

### Page 3

This chapter presents the Tao as emptiness that remains dynamically effective. It is not a fixed thing, but a living source of all becoming. The phrase “not full” means the Tao does not cling to form or possession.

### Page 4

“Blunt sharpness, untangle disorder, temper brightness, and join the dust” describes a way of easing extremes and conflict without imposing another extreme. “Deep and still, as if it were there” points to Dao’s quiet persistence. “Seeming to precede the Lord” places Dao before the very idea of a commanding deity, rather than turning it into another god who issues orders.

## Key Terms and Notes

### Page 5

**The Tao as empty vessel**: The Tao is not a solid substance but a mode of emptiness that can contain everything.

**Not full**: The Tao does not become stagnant or self-satisfied; its power lies in remaining open and receptive.

**Blend with the dust**: The Tao does not seek special distinction; it meets the world in humility and harmony.

## Main Text

### Page 6

This chapter shows that the deepest source of reality is not a creator issuing commands, but a silent emptiness that becomes the stage on which all things appear. The path is not to make oneself brighter or more forceful, but to soften one’s sharpness and live in humble harmony with the world.

## Return to the Original

### Page 7

This chapter does not present the Tao as a god to be worshipped, but as the ultimate ground of being and becoming. Its power is quiet, its greatness is hidden, and its effectiveness lies in not insisting on itself.
