# 第005-1章：天地不仁

## 原文

### 第1页

天地不仁，以万物为刍狗；圣人不仁，以百姓为刍狗。天地之间，其犹橐籥乎？虚而不屈，动而愈出。多言数穷，不如守中。

### 第2页

天地不仁，是说它并不以“仁爱”之心偏护一物，而是按自然法则运作；圣人亦不以私人情感去收拢众人，而是以无私的方式治理。所谓“刍狗”，是古代祭礼中用来象征暂时性供奉的草狗，祭毕即弃，不因情意而妄加珍惜。天地之间如同橐籥，空而不竭，鼓动则气出愈多；言语过多则反致穷困，不如守住内在的中道。

## 释义

### 第3页

“天地不仁”并非说自然残酷，而是说天地不以人的偏爱、恩怨来区分万物。草木鸟兽各自生长、成形、衰败，没有哪一物能因受天地偏爱而免于变化。

### 第4页

“圣人不仁”则是说，治理不应随个人亲疏而改变尺度。这种“不仁”不是冷酷，而是不让一己之爱代替公共判断，使亲近者与疏远者仍受同一秩序约束。

## 术语与考据简注

### 第5页

**刍狗**：古代祭礼中常用的草扎狗形，祭祀完毕便弃置，象征祭礼的功用是暂时性的，不应执著于物。

**橐籥**：古代风箱，空而不满，鼓动时气息愈出，意在说明虚实相生、动静相成。

**守中**：保持内心的平衡与稳住，不为外在多言、争辩、欲求所耗。

## 正文

### 第6页

这一章常被读成“无情”或“冷峻”，但它谈的是不偏私。刍狗在祭礼开始前受到安置，礼毕后便被撤去；它的待遇随礼仪阶段而变，并不因人的眷恋而永久保留。

### 第7页

同样地，天地对万物的态度，不是慈母式地偏护，而是让万物各自成其所成。圣人治理天下，也不是靠施恩布德、以私人情感维系秩序，而是退到“无为”与“守中”的位置，让秩序自然形成。

### 第8页

“橐籥”这一比喻尤其重要。风箱腹中空虚，才能在开合之间送出气流。人的言语若只求压过别人，越说越容易离开问题本身；“守中”不是拒绝说话，而是在开口之前先稳住判断。

## 回扣

### 第9页

本章并非教人变得冷漠，而是提醒我们：真正的力量不是建立在偏爱、施恩、喧哗与执著之上，而是在于保持空明、守住中道、让万物各行其道。

### 第10页

“天地不仁”与“圣人不仁”，其核心都在于“不以私心为中心”。这与《道德经》一贯强调的“无为而治”“虚而不盈”是一脉相承的。

# Chapter 005-1: Heaven and Earth Are Not Benevolent

## Original Text

### Page 1

Heaven and earth do not act from (the impulse of) any wish to be benevolent; they deal with all things as the dogs of grass are dealt with. The sages do not act from (any wish to be) benevolent; they deal with the people as the dogs of grass are dealt with.

### Page 2

May not the space between heaven and earth be compared to a bellows?

'Tis emptied, yet it loses not its power;
'Tis moved again, and sends forth air the more.
Much speech to swift exhaustion lead we see;
Your inner being guard, and keep it free.

## Interpretation

### Page 3

This chapter does not say that nature is cruel. It says that heaven and earth do not distinguish among things according to human affection or resentment. Plants and animals grow, take form, and pass away; no creature is exempted from change because nature happens to favor it.

### Page 4

The sage likewise does not alter the public measure according to personal closeness. This is not coldness. It means that private affection does not replace public judgment, so that those near and far remain subject to the same order.

## Key Terms and Notes

### Page 5

**Straw dogs**: In ancient ritual usage, straw dogs were temporary objects used in sacrifice and then discarded. The point is not attachment but function.

**Bellows**: An instrument that remains empty yet produces ever more air when worked. The image suggests that emptiness and movement are the source of power.

**Guard the inner being**: Keep the mind centered and undistracted, avoiding excessive speech and agitation.

## Main Text

### Page 6

The straw dog makes the point tangible. It is carefully arranged before the rite and removed when the rite is over. Its treatment changes with the stage of the ceremony; personal attachment does not preserve it forever.

### Page 7

In the same way, Heaven and Earth do not shelter one thing as a favored child while abandoning the rest. The sage does not hold society together through personal favors, but steps back from private attachment so that a common order can take shape.

### Page 8

The bellows works because its hollow chamber leaves room for air to move. Speech has a similar limit: when it is used chiefly to overpower others, it soon loses sight of the question. Guarding the center does not mean refusing to speak, but steadying one’s judgment before doing so.

## Return to the Original

### Page 9

Thus the chapter teaches a form of governance and cultivation that is neither sentimental nor harsh. It is free from private attachment, yet still responsive to the needs of the whole.

### Page 10

This chapter does not encourage indifference. It teaches a disciplined form of non-attachment: to act without clinging, to govern without favoritism, and to preserve the inner center rather than waste oneself in endless speech and striving.
